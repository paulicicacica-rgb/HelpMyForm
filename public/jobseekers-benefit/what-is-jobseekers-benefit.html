💶 What Is JB

What Is Jobseeker's Benefit in Ireland?

Jobseeker's Benefit (JB) is a weekly payment for people who become unemployed and have enough PRSI contributions. Unlike Jobseeker's Allowance, it is not means-tested — your income and savings do not affect whether you qualify or how much you receive.

⏱ 6 min read · ✓ Updated 2026 · 🇮🇪 Ireland

The key difference from Jobseeker's Allowance

Jobseeker's Benefit and Jobseeker's Allowance are often confused. They are separate payments with different rules.

Jobseeker's Benefit — the short version

  • Based on your PRSI contributions — you must have paid enough PRSI to qualify
  • Not means-tested — your income, savings, and partner's earnings do not affect the payment
  • Time-limited — you receive it for either 9 months or 6 months, then it stops
  • Paid at a flat weekly rate (with additions for dependants)

Jobseeker's Allowance — the difference

  • Based on a means test — your income and savings are assessed
  • No PRSI requirement — you can claim even with no work history in Ireland
  • No time limit — you can receive it indefinitely while you meet the conditions

Most people who have recently lost a job claim Jobseeker's Benefit first, because it is not means-tested. When JB runs out, they can claim Jobseeker's Allowance — subject to the means test.

Who it is for

Jobseeker's Benefit is designed for people who have been working and paying PRSI, then become unemployed. It is a contribution-based entitlement — you have paid into the system and JB is the return on those contributions when you need it.

It applies to employees who are made redundant, whose contract ends, or who are laid off. Self-employed people who paid Class S PRSI may also qualify — though the rules differ slightly. See the PRSI contributions guide for full details.

The three conditions

1. Enough PRSI contributions

You need a minimum number of paid PRSI contributions and qualifying contributions in the relevant tax years. The exact requirements are in the PRSI contributions guide.

2. Unemployed, available, and seeking work

Same as Jobseeker's Allowance — you must be fully or substantially unemployed (under 4 days per week), available to take up full-time work, and genuinely seeking employment.

3. Under pension age

Once you reach State Pension age (66), you claim the State Pension instead of Jobseeker's Benefit.
