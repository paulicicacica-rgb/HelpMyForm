Jobseeker's Benefit Ireland — Complete Guide 2026

Jobseeker's Benefit is a PRSI-based payment for people who become unemployed. It is not means-tested. Your income, savings, and partner's earnings have no effect on it.

6–9 monthsDuration
104 weeksMinimum PRSI needed

Jobseeker's Benefit (JB) is paid by the Department of Social Protection to people who become unemployed and have sufficient PRSI contributions. It replaced the older Unemployment Benefit name but the structure is the same: you paid in through PRSI while working, and this is the payment you receive when you lose your job.

JB is paid for either 6 months or 9 months depending on how many PRSI contributions you have paid. When it ends, it stops automatically. If you are still unemployed, you can move to Jobseeker's Allowance — but you need to apply for it yourself. There is no automatic transfer.
