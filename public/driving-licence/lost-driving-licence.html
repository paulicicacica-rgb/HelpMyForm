Lost or Stolen Driving Licence — How to Get a Replacement

Losing your driving licence is stressful — but getting a replacement in Ireland is straightforward. Here's the full process, what it costs, and how long it takes.

A lost licence does not affect your driving entitlementsLosing the physical card doesn't mean you've lost your licence. Your driving entitlements are recorded on the NDLS system. You just need a replacement card to prove them.

How to get a replacement licence

1

Report it stolen (if stolen)

If your licence was stolen, report it to An Garda Síochána and get a crime reference number. You'll need this if there's any dispute about fraudulent use of your licence details.

2

Apply for a replacement

You can apply online at ndls.ie if your personal details haven't changed and you meet the eligibility criteria for online applications. Otherwise, attend an NDLS centre in person.

3

Pay €35

The replacement fee is €35. This is the same fee regardless of how many times you've lost your licence.

4

Receive your replacement

Your replacement licence card is posted to your registered address within 5–10 working days. You'll receive a receipt/confirmation at the point of application — keep this in case you're asked by Gardaí.

Can you drive while waiting for your replacement?

Technically, you should carry your licence when driving. However, if you've reported the loss and have a receipt or confirmation of your replacement application, Gardaí will generally accept this as evidence of a valid licence. Keep your application confirmation with you when driving.

If your licence is about to expire, renew — don't just replaceIf your lost licence was close to expiry, it's better to renew (€55) than replace (€35) — you'll get a fresh 10-year licence instead of a replacement card for the remaining time.
