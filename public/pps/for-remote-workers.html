PPS Number for Remote Workers in Ireland

Working remotely in Ireland — whether for an Irish company, a foreign company, or as a freelancer — requires a PPS number. The tricky part is knowing what evidence of need to bring when your employer isn't Irish.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

Your situation and what it means for your PPS application

Working remotely for an Irish employer

Standard employment. Your employer asks for your PPS number to put you on payroll. Bring a letter of employment or job offer from your Irish employer as evidence of need.

Working remotely for a foreign employer from Ireland

You're living in Ireland but your employer is based abroad. If you're resident in Ireland for tax purposes, you still need a PPS number — and you're liable for Irish income tax. Bring a letter from your foreign employer confirming you work for them from Ireland, and a letter from Revenue confirming you need to register.

Digital nomad / freelancer in Ireland

If you're based in Ireland and earning income here — even from abroad — you need a PPS number to register with Revenue. A signed client contract or a letter from Revenue requesting your registration is your evidence of need.

Documents to bring

Passport or ID (+ IRP for non-EU)Standard identity requirements — same as all other PPS applications.
Proof of address in IrelandUtility bill, bank statement, or letter from landlord dated within last 3 months.
Evidence of needFor foreign employer: employment letter confirming you work from Ireland. For freelancers: client contract or Revenue registration letter. For Irish employers: job offer or employment contract.
Register with Revenue after getting your PPS numberIf you're working for a foreign employer or as a freelancer, you're responsible for filing your own tax return in Ireland — Revenue won't receive PAYE from your employer automatically. Register at myaccount.revenue.ie once you have your PPS number.
