PPS Application Delayed — What to Do

If you had your appointment and still haven't received your PPS number after 5–10 working days, it's time to follow up. Here's what to do step by step.

⏱ 3 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland
What counts as a delay?If it's been less than 5 working days since your appointment, it's not a delay — some offices post the number within a few days. If it's been more than 10 working days and you've received no letter and no contact, follow the steps below.

What to do if your PPS is taking too long

  • 1

    Check your post carefully

    Your PPS number arrives by post in a plain envelope from the Department of Social Protection. Check that it hasn't been delivered to a different address or lost in the post.

  • 2

    Call the Intreo office where you had your appointment

    Call the specific office that handled your appointment. Have your full name, date of birth, and the date of your appointment ready. Ask them to check the status of your application.

  • 3

    Call the Department of Social Protection helpline

    If the local office can't help, call the Department of Social Protection on 071 919 3302. Explain you had your PPS appointment, give your details, and ask for a status update.

  • 4

    If asked — provide any missing documents quickly

    Sometimes a delay means your application is on hold pending a missing document. If they tell you this, get the required document to them as fast as possible — by post or in person.
