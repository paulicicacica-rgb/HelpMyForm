What is a PPS Number?

A Personal Public Service (PPS) Number is your unique ID number in Ireland. It is 7 digits followed by 1 or 2 letters — for example, 1234567A.

You need it to work, pay tax, access healthcare, apply for social welfare, open a bank account, and use most government services. It is one of the first things you should get when you arrive in Ireland.

Good to know You do not need a PPS number to start looking for work or to accept a job offer. You can start working without it while your application is being processed.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  • 1

    Create a MyGovID account

    Go to mygovid.ie and create a basic account. You only need an email address to do this.

  • 2

    Book your appointment online

    Go to mywelfare.ie, log in, and apply for a PPS Number. You will choose a date, time and location for your in-person appointment.

  • 3

    Gather your documents

    You must bring specific documents to your appointment. See the full list below. Do not attend without them — your application will not be processed.

  • 4

    Attend your appointment in person

    Go to your local PPS Number Allocation Centre on the date and time you booked. Bring all your documents. The appointment itself is short — usually 10–15 minutes.

  • 5

    Receive your PPS number by post

    Your PPS number will be sent to your address by letter within 1–2 weeks. Make sure your address is correct when you apply.

Documents You Need to Bring

You must bring originals — not photocopies. You need to prove three things:

1. Photo ID (Proof of Identity) Valid passport, national identity card, or Irish driving licence. Must be current and not expired.
2. Proof of Address A utility bill, bank statement, or official letter showing your name and Irish address. Must be dated within the last 3 months. If you are staying with someone else, get a bill from the person you live with plus a signed note from them confirming you live there.
3. Reason why you need a PPS Number An employment contract, letter from employer, social welfare application reference, or letter from Revenue. Looking for work is NOT accepted as a reason.
Important If you arrived from Ukraine under EU Temporary Protection, do not apply online. Go directly to your nearest PPS Number Allocation Centre — special arrangements are in place for you.

Where to Apply

You must apply in person at a PPS Number Allocation Centre. There are centres in every county in Ireland — Dublin, Cork, Limerick, Galway, and all other areas.

You can find your nearest centre when booking your appointment at mywelfare.ie, or by calling your local Intreo office.

Already have a PPS number? If you lived in Ireland before, you already have one. Check your payslips, medical card, tax documents, or any letter from a government office. Contact your local Intreo centre if you cannot find it.

Common Questions

Can I apply before I arrive in Ireland?

No. You must be resident in Ireland to apply. Wait until you have arrived and have an Irish address before starting your application.

How long does it take to get a PPS number?

Usually 1 to 2 weeks after your in-person appointment. Your number will arrive by post at the address you provided.

Is it free to get a PPS number?

Yes. Applying for a PPS number is completely free. You do not need to pay anything.

I don't have a utility bill — what can I use for proof of address?

If you are staying with someone, get a household bill from the person you live with. Ask them to write a note on the bill saying you live at that address and sign it. This is accepted.
