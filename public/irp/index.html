Irish Residence Permit (IRP) — Complete Guide 2026

The IRP is the official proof of a non-EEA national's permission to live in Ireland. First registration, renewal, stamp types, changing employer, and the path to long-term residency — all explained.

The Irish Residence Permit (IRP) replaced the older GNIB card in 2020. It is issued by the Garda National Immigration Bureau to non-EEA nationals who have been granted permission to remain in Ireland — for work, study, family, or other purposes.

The stamp type on your IRP determines what you can do in Ireland: whether you can work, what services you can access, and how long your permission lasts. Understanding your stamp type is the foundation of navigating Irish immigration.
