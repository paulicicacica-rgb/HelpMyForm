🔄 Renewing Your IRP

How to Renew Your IRP in Ireland

IRP cards expire and must be renewed before the expiry date. An expired IRP puts your immigration status at risk. Here is how the renewal process works.

⏱ 6 min read · ✓ Updated 2026 · 🇮🇪 Ireland

When to start the renewal process

Start at least 4–6 weeks before your IRP expiry date. In Dublin, appointment slots fill up quickly — book as soon as the renewal window opens. Outside Dublin, contact your Garda station early.

Do not let your IRP expireOnce expired, your immigration permission has technically lapsed. This can affect your ability to work legally, open bank accounts, and access services. One late appointment can create weeks of complications. Renew on time.

Renewal step by step

  • 1

    Book your appointment

    Dublin: inisonline.jahs.ie. Outside Dublin: your local Garda station. Book as early as possible.

  • 2

    Gather your documents

    Current IRP (even if expired), valid passport, proof of address, and evidence your permission continues — employer letter, updated contract, or college enrollment.

  • 3

    Attend and pay

    Renewal fee is €300 for most categories. The officer checks your documents and processes the renewal.

  • 4

    Receive your new card

    Dublin: usually issued on the day. Outside Dublin: posted within a few weeks.

If your circumstances changed

If your job, study situation, or family status changed since your last registration, your renewal may result in a different stamp type. Bring full documentation of your current situation. If unsure, check with INIS or an immigration adviser before attending.
