🔍 Lost / Stolen IRP

Lost or Stolen IRP — What to Do

If your IRP is lost or stolen, report it immediately and apply for a replacement. Without the physical card you cannot prove your immigration status to employers or services.

⏱ 6 min read · ✓ Updated 2026 · 🇮🇪 Ireland

Report it first

Go to your local Garda station and report the loss or theft. Get a Garda report or crime reference number — you will need this at the replacement appointment. If stolen, a report is especially important as the card contains biometric data.

Getting a replacement

  • 1

    Garda report

    Visit your local Garda station. Report the loss or theft and get a reference number or written report.

  • 2

    Book replacement appointment

    Dublin: inisonline.jahs.ie (select replacement option). Outside Dublin: local Garda station.

  • 3

    Attend with documents

    Bring: valid passport, Garda report, proof of address, and the replacement fee (€300).

  • 4

    Receive replacement

    New IRP issued with same stamp type and new expiry. Your immigration permission is unaffected by the loss of the physical card.

Your permission is on the system — not the cardThe GNIB system records your permission. The physical card is just the token. Your status does not change when you lose the card — but proving it becomes difficult without one, which is why a fast replacement matters.

While waiting for the replacement

A letter from the GNIB confirming your registration can be used as temporary evidence of status. Contact the GNIB or your local Garda station to request one while the replacement is being processed.
