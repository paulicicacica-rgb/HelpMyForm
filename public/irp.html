What is an IRP Card?

An IRP (Irish Residence Permit) card is your official proof that you have permission to live in Ireland. It replaced the old GNIB card. Every non-EEA national living in Ireland must have one and must keep it up to date.

Your IRP card shows your name, nationality, permission type (stamp), and the date your permission expires. You must renew it before it expires — do not wait until the last day.

Renew early — do not let it expireBook your renewal appointment at least 4–6 weeks before your card expires. Appointments can be hard to get and letting your card expire can cause serious problems with your immigration status.

Types of Permission (Stamps)

Your IRP card has a stamp number that shows what type of permission you have. The documents you need for renewal depend on your stamp type:

Stamp 1

Work Permit

For people with an employment permit. You need your current work permit and a letter from your employer.

Stamp 2

Student

For students enrolled in a full-time course. You need your current enrolment letter and evidence of attendance.

Stamp 3

No recourse to public funds

For those not working or studying. You need evidence of sufficient funds to support yourself.

Stamp 4

Long-term residency

For those with leave to remain or long-term residency. You need your permission letter from the Irish Naturalisation and Immigration Service (INIS).

Stamp 4 EUFAM

Family member of EU citizen

For non-EEA family members of EU citizens. You need evidence of the EU citizen's residence and your relationship.

How to Renew — Step by Step

  • 1

    Book your appointment online

    Go to inisonline.jahs.ie and book your renewal appointment. In Dublin, appointments are managed by the Burgh Quay Registration Office. Outside Dublin, go to your local Garda immigration office.

  • 2

    Pay the €300 fee

    Pay the renewal fee online when booking. Keep your payment confirmation — you will need it at your appointment. Children under 18 are free.

  • 3

    Gather your documents

    Collect all required documents before your appointment. See the full list below. Missing documents means your renewal cannot be processed on the day.

  • 4

    Attend your appointment in person

    Arrive on time with all your original documents — not photocopies. The appointment itself is usually 10–20 minutes.

  • 5

    Receive your new IRP card

    In Dublin, your new card is usually issued at the appointment. Outside Dublin, it may be posted to you within a few days.

Documents You Must Bring

Bring originals — not photocopies. The officer will check and return them.

Valid passportMust be valid for at least 6 months beyond your renewal date. If your passport has expired, renew it first.
Current IRP cardYour existing card that is due for renewal
Payment confirmationProof that you have paid the €300 renewal fee
Proof of permission to remainThis depends on your stamp type — employment permit, student enrolment letter, INIS letter, etc.
Proof of addressA utility bill or bank statement showing your current Irish address, dated within the last 3 months
Proof of income or fundsRecent payslips, bank statements, or evidence of sufficient funds to support yourself
Check your stamp typeDifferent stamp types require different supporting documents. Check the Immigration Service website or your approval letter to confirm exactly what you need for your specific stamp.

Common Questions

How early should I book my renewal?

Book at least 4 to 6 weeks before your card expires. Appointments fill up quickly, especially in Dublin. Do not leave it to the last week.

What happens if my IRP expires before I get an appointment?

If your card expires and you have proof you tried to book an appointment in time (a booking confirmation), you are generally considered to be in a legal position. However, this can cause problems — book as early as possible.

My passport has expired — can I still renew my IRP?

No. You must renew your passport before renewing your IRP. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your IRP renewal date.

Is the €300 fee refundable?

No. The €300 fee is non-refundable, even if your application is refused. Make sure you have all your documents before paying and attending.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Leaving it too late to bookAppointments in Dublin can take weeks to become available. Book as soon as your card is within 2 months of expiry — not the week before.
Expired passportYour passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your renewal date. Renew your passport first if needed — you cannot renew IRP without it.
Bringing photocopies instead of originalsAll documents must be originals. Photocopies are not accepted at the appointment.
Not bringing proof of the underlying permissionYou need proof of what allows you to stay — work permit, student letter, stamp etc. Without this the renewal cannot proceed.
Paying the €300 and then missing your appointmentThe fee is non-refundable. If you need to cancel, rebook before missing your slot.
