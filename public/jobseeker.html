What is Jobseeker's Allowance?

Jobseeker's Allowance (JA) is a weekly payment from the Irish government for people who are unemployed and looking for work. It is paid by the Department of Social Protection through your local Intreo centre.

There are two types of Jobseeker payment in Ireland:

Contribution based
Jobseeker's Benefit

Based on PRSI contributions. If you worked and paid PRSI in Ireland, apply for this first.

Means tested
Jobseeker's Allowance

Based on your income and savings. For those who don't qualify for Benefit or whose Benefit has run out.

Not sure which one?If you have worked in Ireland and paid PRSI, apply for Jobseeker's Benefit first. If you have not worked here or your Benefit runs out, you will be assessed for Jobseeker's Allowance.

Who Can Apply?

To qualify for Jobseeker's Allowance you must:

Be unemployed — fully or partially (working less than 4 days a week)
Be available for and actively seeking full-time work
Be habitually resident in Ireland — meaning you live here and intend to stay
Have a PPS number
Pass a means test — your income and savings must be below a certain level
Immigrants — Habitual Residence ConditionTo qualify, you must pass the Habitual Residence Condition (HRC). This means proving you have strong ties to Ireland and intend to live here long-term. Recent arrivals may find this harder to satisfy. Bring evidence of how long you have been in Ireland and your intention to stay.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  • 1

    Go to your Intreo centre immediately

    Do not wait. Go to your nearest Intreo centre on the first day you are unemployed. Your payment starts from the date you sign on — not the date you lost your job.

  • 2

    Fill in the UP1 form

    You will be given a UP1 application form at the Intreo office. Fill it in carefully — every section must be completed. You can also download it from gov.ie before you go.

  • 3

    Bring all your documents

    See the full list below. Missing documents will delay your application. Bring originals — not photocopies.

  • 4

    Sign on regularly

    After your initial application, you will be required to sign on regularly — usually every week or two weeks — to confirm you are still unemployed and looking for work.

  • 5

    Receive your weekly payment

    If approved, you will receive a weekly payment. You can choose to be paid by post, into a bank account, or at the post office.

Apply on day oneYour payment starts from the date you sign on — not the date you became unemployed. Every day you delay is money you lose. Go to Intreo on your first day without work.

Documents You Need

PPS NumberEssential — you cannot apply without it
Photo IDPassport, national ID card, or Irish driving licence
Proof of addressUtility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months
Proof of unemploymentA letter from your employer confirming your employment has ended, or your P45/final payslip
Bank account detailsIBAN and BIC for your Irish bank account
Evidence of habitual residenceIf you are an immigrant — proof of how long you have lived in Ireland, such as previous correspondence, lease agreements, or utility bills
PayslipsRecent payslips from your last job — helps with means assessment

Common Questions

Can immigrants apply for Jobseeker's Allowance?

Yes, if you are legally resident in Ireland and pass the Habitual Residence Condition (HRC). You must show you have strong ties to Ireland and intend to live here long-term. Bring as much evidence of your time in Ireland as possible.

How much will I receive?

The maximum personal rate is €232 per week (2026). You may receive less depending on your means assessment. Additional amounts may be available for a partner or children.

What is the means test?

The Intreo officer will look at your income, savings, and your partner's income to decide how much you should receive. Any income you have reduces your payment.

Can I work part-time and still claim?

Yes. If you work less than 4 days a week you may still qualify. The days you work will reduce your payment — you are only paid for the days you are unemployed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Waiting to register — even one day costs you moneyYour payment starts from your registration date. Go to Intreo the same day you become unemployed, not days later.
Not bringing proof of unemploymentYou need a letter from your employer confirming your job ended, or your P45. Without it your application cannot be processed.
Not having a PPS number readyYou cannot apply without a PPS number. If you don't have one, apply for it immediately — but note that this will delay your Jobseeker payment.
Not re-registering regularlyYou must sign on regularly to keep receiving payment. Missing a sign-on date can stop your payment entirely.
Not reporting part-time workIf you start part-time work while on Jobseeker's you must tell your Intreo centre. Failing to report it is fraud.
