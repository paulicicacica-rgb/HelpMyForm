How to Fill in the HAP Form in Ireland

What is HAP?

HAP stands for Housing Assistance Payment. It is an Irish government scheme that helps people who cannot afford rent. Under HAP, your local council pays your rent directly to your landlord every month.

You pay a weekly contribution to the council based on your income — usually much less than the full rent. The council pays the rest to your landlord.

Good to knowIf you are working full-time, you can still receive HAP. Unlike older schemes, HAP does not stop when you get a job or increase your hours.

Who Can Apply?

To qualify for HAP you must meet all of the following:

You are on your local authority's social housing waiting list
You have a legal right to remain in Ireland long-term
Your household income is below the limit for your area
You have found a private rental property within HAP limits
Your landlord agrees to take part in HAP
ImportantIf you arrived under Ukraine Temporary Protection, you are not currently eligible for HAP as your right to remain is temporary.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  • 1

    Get on the social housing list first

    If you are not already on your local authority's housing waiting list, you must apply for social housing support first. Contact your local council to do this.

  • 2

    Find a suitable property

    Find a private rental where the rent is within HAP limits for your area and household size. Your landlord must agree to participate.

  • 3

    Request the HAP application form

    Contact your local authority to get the form. Only request it after you have found your accommodation. You can also apply online at hap.ie.

  • 4

    Fill in Part A — your section

    Complete the tenant section with your personal details, household information, income, and property details. Every field must be filled in — incomplete forms are rejected.

  • 5

    Landlord fills in Part B

    Your landlord completes and signs their section, including bank details for payment. They can submit this separately if needed.

  • 6

    Submit with all documents

    Return the completed form with all required documents to your local authority as quickly as possible. HAP payments only start from the date your application is approved — not the date you moved in.

The HAP Form Has Two Parts

Your section
Part A — Tenant

Your personal details, household members, income, and property you are renting.

Landlord section
Part B — Landlord

Landlord details, property info, rent amount, and bank account for payments.

Do not submit an incomplete formBoth parts must be completed before submission. Incomplete forms will not be accepted.

Documents You Need

Photo IDPassport, national ID card or Irish driving licence
PPS NumberYou and all household members must have one
Proof of incomeRecent payslips, social welfare letter, or employment details summary
Tenancy agreementSigned by both you and your landlord
Social housing reference numberFrom your local authority — confirms you are on the housing list
Birth certificatesFor any children in your household

Common Questions

How much will I have to pay?

You pay a weekly rent contribution to your local council based on your household income — usually around 12% of your net income. The council pays the rest directly to your landlord.

Can I work and still get HAP?

Yes. HAP has no income limit once you have been approved. If your income increases, your contribution may change but you will not lose HAP.

What if my landlord refuses to take HAP?

Landlords cannot legally refuse to rent to someone because they are on HAP. This is discrimination under Irish law. You can report it to the Residential Tenancies Board (RTB).

How long does it take to get approved?

It varies by local authority. Once a valid complete application is received it can take a few weeks. Apply as soon as you move in — payments only start from the approval date.

Can I get help filling in the HAP form?

Yes — that is exactly what HelpMyForm is for. Take a photo of your HAP form and get field-by-field guidance in your language, for free.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Applying before getting on the social housing listYou must be on your local authority's social housing waiting list before you can apply for HAP. This step comes first.
Leaving sections of the form blankIncomplete forms are automatically rejected. Every field must be filled in — including your landlord's section.
Waiting too long to submit after moving inHAP payments only start from your approval date, not your move-in date. Submit as soon as possible after moving in.
Not telling your landlord about the scheme firstYour landlord must agree to participate in HAP. Confirm this before applying — not all landlords are registered.
Missing the rent thresholdIf your rent is above the local HAP limit, your application may be complicated. Check the limits for your area before signing a lease.
Official SourceThis guide is based on information from the Housing Assistance Payment scheme. You can view official HAP details at hap.ie and your local authority website.
