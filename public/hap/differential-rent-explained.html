HAP Differential Rent — What You Actually Pay

When you're on HAP, you don't pay rent directly to your landlord. You pay a differential rent to your council. Here's exactly how that amount is calculated and what changes it.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

How differential rent works

Under HAP, your council pays your landlord the full agreed rent each month. In return, you pay a weekly contribution — called differential rent — directly to the council. The council covers the gap.

Your differential rent is calculated as a percentage of your net household income. The higher your income, the more you contribute — but HAP still tops up whatever you don't cover, up to the HAP rent limit.

Each council sets its own rate The exact percentage varies by council. Dublin City Council, Cork City Council, and others each have their own differential rent scheme. Ask your council for their specific calculation.

What changes your contribution

  • 1

    Your income goes up

    Pay rise, new job, more hours — your contribution increases at the next annual review. You must report income changes to your council. Don't wait for the review — report it when it happens.

  • 2

    Your income goes down

    Lost your job, reduced hours, reduced social welfare — your contribution decreases. Contact the council immediately and ask for your differential rent to be recalculated. Don't keep paying the higher amount.

  • 3

    Household composition changes

    A partner moves in or out, a child is born, a dependent leaves — these all affect the calculation. Notify your council of any changes to who lives in the household.

  • 4

    Annual review

    Every year the council reviews your income and recalculates your contribution. They'll write to you asking for updated income documents. Respond promptly — delays can cause arrears.

Missing contributions causes problemsIf you fall behind on your differential rent, the council can suspend HAP payments to your landlord. Set up a direct debit from day one and never miss a payment.
