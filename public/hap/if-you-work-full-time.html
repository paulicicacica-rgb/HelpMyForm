Can I Get HAP If I Work Full Time?

A lot of people assume HAP is only for people who are unemployed. It's not. Working full time doesn't disqualify you — it just changes how much you contribute.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland
Short answer
Yes — you can get HAP while working full time. Your income affects your weekly contribution, not your eligibility.

How it works when you're employed

HAP is designed to help people who can't afford private rent, regardless of whether they work or not. When you're employed, the council calculates a differential rent — the amount you pay to the council each week based on your income.

The higher your income, the higher your weekly contribution. But HAP still covers the gap between what you pay and what your landlord is owed — up to the HAP rent limit for your area.

Example: Full-time worker on €600/week gross

Gross weekly wage€600
Income disregard (employment)−€75
Assessable income€525
Your weekly contribution (~15%)~€79/week
HAP covers the rest up to rent limit✓
Employment income disregard Many councils apply a partial disregard to employment income — meaning not all of your wages are counted. This is to encourage working. Ask your council what disregard applies in your case.

What you need to do differently as an employee

  • 1

    Bring 3 months of payslips

    Your last 3 payslips from every employer. If you have two jobs, bring payslips from both. The council needs to see your gross income — the figure before tax.

  • 2

    Declare all income sources

    If you receive any social welfare top-ups alongside your wages — like Working Family Payment — declare those too. All household income must be on the form.

  • 3

    Report income changes

    If your wages change after you're approved — pay rise, change of hours, new job — you must notify the council. Your contribution will be recalculated. Failing to report changes can cause problems later.
