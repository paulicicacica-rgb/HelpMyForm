How Long Does HAP Take to Process?

Processing times vary widely depending on your council and how complete your application is. Here's what's normal — and what to do if yours is taking too long.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

Typical processing times

2–4 weeks

Smaller counties — straightforward applications

Complete applications with all documents submitted correctly. Councils like Leitrim, Roscommon, and Longford typically process faster.

4–8 weeks

Medium councils — standard wait

Normal range for most councils when the application is complete. Worth a check-in call after 6 weeks if you've heard nothing.

8–16 weeks

Dublin and high-demand areas

Dublin City Council and other high-volume councils can take 3–4 months or more. This is unfortunately normal but worth following up actively.

Keep paying rent while you wait HAP does not backdate payments. Your landlord must be paid during the waiting period — HAP will only pay from the date of approval going forward.

What makes applications take longer

  • 1

    Missing documents

    The most common cause. The council puts the application on hold waiting for you to send what's missing — sometimes without contacting you. Always submit a complete application.

  • 2

    Self-employment income

    Harder to verify than payslips. Self-employed applications consistently take longer because the means test requires more analysis.

  • 3

    Incomplete landlord section

    If your landlord missed a field or didn't sign correctly, the application stalls until it's corrected.

  • 4

    High application volumes

    Some councils are simply overwhelmed. Not much you can do except follow up regularly and politely.
