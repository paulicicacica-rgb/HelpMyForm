HAP Application Delayed — What to Do Right Now

HAP applications can take weeks or months. But there's a difference between normal processing time and something actually being stuck. Here's how to tell — and what to do.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

What's a normal waiting time?

Processing times vary significantly by council. Some process HAP in 2–4 weeks. Others, particularly in Dublin, can take 2–3 months or longer during busy periods.

2–4 weeks

Normal in smaller counties. Application is moving through the system.

4–8 weeks

Common in busier councils. Worth a check-in call but not unusual.

8+ weeks

Time to follow up actively. Something may be missing or stuck.

Why applications get stuck

  • 1

    A document is missing

    The council puts the application on hold and waits for you to send the missing item. They may not contact you — the application just sits there. This is the most common reason for long delays.

  • 2

    The landlord section is incomplete

    If your landlord missed a field or didn't sign correctly, the council can't process until it's corrected. They may contact your landlord directly — or wait for you to sort it.

  • 3

    Means test is taking time

    If your income situation is complex — multiple jobs, self-employment, variable hours — the means test takes longer to calculate. This is normal but can add weeks.

  • 4

    High volume at the council

    Some councils are overwhelmed with applications. There's not much you can do here except follow up politely and regularly.

What to do if your application is delayed

  • 1

    Call your council's HAP section directly

    Don't email — call. Ask for the status of your application and whether anything is missing. Have your application reference number ready. Write down the name of who you spoke to and what they said.

  • 2

    Ask specifically if documents are missing

    Don't ask "is everything okay?" — ask "is there anything missing from my application?" A direct question gets a direct answer.

  • 3

    Follow up in writing

    After the call, send an email to the HAP section summarising what you discussed. This creates a record and often speeds things up — councils tend to prioritise cases where the applicant is actively engaged.

  • 4

    Contact your local TD or councillor

    If weeks pass with no movement, contact your local councillor or TD. This is completely normal in Ireland and often unblocks stalled applications quickly. It's not complaining — it's using the system as it's designed.

Keep paying your rent while you wait HAP does not pay backdated rent. Keep paying your landlord as normal while the application is being processed. Once approved, HAP pays going forward from the approval date.
