What is a Medical Card?

A Medical Card gives you free access to GP visits, hospital treatment, prescription medicines, dental care, and other health services in Ireland. It is issued by the HSE (Health Service Executive).

There are two types of card — a full Medical Card and a GP Visit Card. The full Medical Card covers more services.

Full cover
Medical Card

Free GP, hospital, prescriptions, dental, optical and more. Based on income.

GP only
GP Visit Card

Free GP visits only. Higher income limit — easier to qualify for.

Good to knowIf you don't qualify for a full Medical Card, you may still qualify for a GP Visit Card, which has a higher income limit.

Who Can Apply?

You can apply if you are ordinarily resident in Ireland — meaning you live here and intend to stay. This includes immigrants, refugees, and EU/EEA citizens living in Ireland.

Eligibility is mainly based on your weekly income after tax. The limits depend on your age and how many people are in your household. As a general guide for 2025:

Single person under 66Income under approximately €184 per week after tax
Couple under 66Income under approximately €266 per week after tax
With childrenHigher limits apply — each child adds to your allowance
Over 70Automatic entitlement — no income test needed
NoteEven if your income is slightly above the limit, you may still qualify if you have high medical expenses, rent, or childcare costs. Always apply and let the HSE assess you.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  • 1

    Apply online (easiest)

    Go to medicalcard.ie and apply online. You will need a MyGovID account or your PPS number. This is the fastest way.

  • 2

    Or apply by paper form

    Download the paper form from hse.ie or pick one up at your local HSE office or GP surgery. Fill it in and post it to the HSE Primary Care Reimbursement Service.

  • 3

    Fill in all your details

    Include details for everyone in your household — name, PPS number, date of birth, and income. Do not leave any section blank.

  • 4

    Attach your documents

    Include proof of income, proof of address, and proof of identity for everyone named on the application.

  • 5

    Submit and wait

    Submit online or post your form. Processing usually takes 3–4 weeks. If approved, your card will arrive by post. If refused, you have the right to appeal.

Documents You Need

PPS NumberFor you and everyone in your household
Proof of identityPassport or national ID card
Proof of addressUtility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months
Proof of incomeRecent payslips (last 3 months), social welfare payment letter, or employment details summary from Revenue
Bank statementsLast 3 months — shows your actual income and outgoings
Children's birth certificatesIf applying for children
Rent or mortgage detailsIf you pay high rent, include this — it may help your application
ImportantIf you are self-employed, you need to provide your most recent tax return (Form 11) and audited accounts if available.

Common Questions

Can immigrants apply for a Medical Card?

Yes. If you are legally resident in Ireland and your income is below the limit, you can apply. This includes EU/EEA citizens, work permit holders, and those with refugee status or leave to remain.

How long does it take to get a Medical Card?

Usually 3 to 4 weeks after submitting a complete application. If your application is urgent due to medical need, you can request a temporary card while it is being processed.

My income is slightly too high — can I still apply?

Yes. Always apply. The HSE considers additional expenses like rent, childcare, and medical costs. Many people with income above the guideline still qualify once all expenses are taken into account.

What if my application is refused?

You can appeal the decision within 30 days. You can also ask the HSE to review your application if your circumstances have changed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not applying because you think you earn too muchAlways apply. The HSE considers rent, childcare and medical expenses on top of income. Many people just over the limit still qualify.
Leaving household members off the formEvery person in your household must be listed, including children. Missing members can delay or invalidate your application.
Not including bank statementsThe HSE requires 3 months of bank statements. Missing financial documents is the most common reason applications are delayed.
Forgetting to include your GP's detailsYou need to name a GP on your application. Your card is linked to that practice.
Not appealing a refusalIf your application is refused you have 30 days to appeal. Many appeals are successful, especially if your circumstances have changed.
