How Long Does a Medical Card Take in Ireland?

A complete medical card application takes around 15 working days to process. Here's what happens during that time, what causes delays, and how to follow up if it's taking too long.

⏱ 3 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

Typical processing times

15 working days

Standard complete application

If all documents are included and your income is clearly within the limits, most applications are decided within 15 working days of receipt.

4–6 weeks

Complex or borderline applications

Applications near the income limit, with multiple income sources, or requiring discretionary consideration take longer — typically 4 to 6 weeks.

6+ weeks

Missing documents or information requests

If the HSE writes to request more information, the clock resets from when you respond. Missing documents are the single biggest cause of delays.

Urgent medical need? Request a temporary cardIf you're waiting for a medical card decision but need healthcare urgently, ask your GP or the HSE about a temporary medical card. These can be issued while your application is being processed in exceptional circumstances.

If it's been more than 4 weeks

  • 1

    Check your post for any HSE letters

    The HSE communicates by post. Check carefully — they may have written asking for additional documents and you may have missed the letter.

  • 2

    Call the HSE PCRS

    Call 0818 22 44 78 (Monday–Friday). Have your PPS number and the date you applied ready. Ask for a status update on your application.

  • 3

    Contact your local TD if still no progress

    If weeks pass with no update, contact a local TD. This is a common and effective route — TDs can make official enquiries that often unstick delayed applications.
