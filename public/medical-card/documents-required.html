Medical Card — Documents Required Ireland 2026

Missing documents are the most common reason medical card applications are delayed or refused. Here's exactly what to include — split by income type.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

Required for everyone

Everyone must include these

PPS numbers for all household membersAdults and children — everyone in the household needs their PPS number included.
Proof of addressUtility bill, bank statement, or official letter dated within the last 3 months at your current Irish address.
Photo ID (if applying by post)Passport or driver's licence. Not required for online applications.

Proof of income — by income type

💼 If you're employed (PAYE)

Last 3 payslipsMust show gross pay, net pay, and all deductions clearly.
Most recent P60 or Employment Detail SummaryAvailable from your employer or from Revenue MyAccount.

💼 If you're self-employed

Most recent audited accounts or tax assessmentShowing your actual net income after business expenses.
Most recent Revenue Notice of AssessmentShowing your declared income for the year.

💶 If you're on social welfare

Recent social welfare payment letterA letter from the DSP confirming the payment type and weekly amount.

🏠 If you're paying rent or mortgage

Rent: Lease agreement or rent bookShowing the monthly rent amount — this is deducted from your assessed income.
Mortgage: Recent mortgage statementShowing monthly repayment amount from your lender.
Medical expenses help your caseIf you have significant ongoing medical costs, include receipts or a letter from your GP or consultant. This supports a discretionary medical card application even if you're slightly above the income limit.
