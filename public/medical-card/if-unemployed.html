💶 Unemployed / Social Welfare

Medical Card if Unemployed or on Social Welfare

If you are receiving Jobseeker's Allowance, One-Parent Family Payment, or other low social welfare payments, you are very likely to qualify for a medical card. Your social welfare payment counts as your income for the assessment.

⏱ 4 min read · ✓ Updated 2026 · 🇮🇪 Ireland

Social welfare payments and the means test

Your weekly social welfare payment is your assessed income. Jobseeker's Allowance is €232 per week in 2026 — below the income limit for a single person (€184 net or roughly equivalent after deductions). Most people on Jobseeker's Allowance qualify.

Jobseeker's Allowance (€232/week)

At this rate, most single people are within the medical card income limit after standard deductions. Apply as soon as you are on Jobseeker's.

One-Parent Family Payment

Recipients of OFP are generally within income limits for a medical card — especially after the child additions are accounted for.

Disability Allowance

DA recipients are typically within limits. If you also have significant medical costs, mention them in your application to strengthen a discretionary card case.

Include your DSP payment letterThe clearest proof of income for social welfare recipients is the letter from the Department of Social Protection confirming your payment type and weekly amount. This is your key document.
