📋 What It Covers

What Does a Medical Card Cover in Ireland?

A medical card covers a wide range of health services in Ireland — all free of charge. Here is the full breakdown of what is and is not included.

⏱ 4 min read · ✓ Updated 2026 · 🇮🇪 Ireland

Services covered by the medical card

🩺 GP visits — unlimited

All consultations with your registered GP, including home visits and out-of-hours emergency calls, are free. No charge for GP letters or referrals.

💊 Prescriptions — all approved medicines

All medicines on the HSE approved list are free. You pay nothing at the pharmacy. Some very specialised medications may not be on the approved list.

🏥 Public hospital care

Inpatient care, outpatient appointments, day procedures, A&E visits, and overnight stays in public hospitals are all free. Private hospitals are not covered.

🦷 Dental services

Basic dental care including examinations, extractions, and some restorations. More complex treatments like implants or orthodontics are not covered.

👁️ Optical services

Eye tests and a contribution towards the cost of glasses. Designer frames are not covered but basic frames are.

👂 Aural services

Hearing tests and basic hearing aids through the HSE aural service.

🤰 Maternity services

Antenatal visits, maternity hospital care, and postnatal checks — including GP maternity visits — are all covered.

🚑 Ambulance service

Emergency ambulance transport is free for medical card holders.

What is NOT covered

Private hospital care, cosmetic procedures, non-approved medicines, most orthodontic work, and specialist private consultations are not covered by the medical card.
