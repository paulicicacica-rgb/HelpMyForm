Medical Card for EU / EEA Citizens in Ireland

EU and EEA citizens living and working in Ireland can apply for a medical card — but you must meet the Habitual Residence Condition. Here's what that means and how to demonstrate it.

⏱ 4 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland
Key requirement for EU citizens
You must meet the Habitual Residence Condition — showing that Ireland is your main country of residence and that you intend to stay. Working and paying taxes in Ireland is strong evidence.

What is the Habitual Residence Condition?

The Habitual Residence Condition (HRC) is a test used by the HSE to determine whether you're entitled to access means-tested public services in Ireland. It's not about citizenship — it's about where your life is centred.

Length of time in IrelandThe longer you've lived in Ireland, the stronger your HRC claim. Generally, 2+ years of continuous residence is very strong evidence.
Employment in IrelandWorking and paying PRSI in Ireland is the strongest single factor for HRC. It demonstrates economic integration.
Centre of interestWhere your family lives, where your children go to school, where you have a bank account — all show Ireland is your main base.
Future intentionsEvidence that you intend to remain in Ireland — lease agreements, employment contracts, school enrolment — all support your HRC claim.

Documents to include for EU applicants

Passport or EU identity cardYour valid EU/EEA identity document.
PPS numberFor yourself and all household members.
Proof of address in IrelandUtility bill, bank statement, or lease agreement dated within last 3 months.
Proof of employment or incomePayslips, employment contract, or social welfare letter confirming your payments.
Evidence of habitual residenceLease agreement, utility bills showing consistent address history, children's school enrolment letters, or employer letter confirming employment in Ireland.
Just arrived in Ireland?If you've arrived recently and are starting work, apply once you have your PPS number and at least one payslip. Your employment contract and lease agreement are sufficient HRC evidence at this stage.
