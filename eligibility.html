Rent Supplement — Am I Eligible?

There are four main eligibility rules. You need to pass all four. Here's what each one means and how to know if you qualify.

⏱ 5 min read✓ Updated 2026🇮🇪 Ireland

The four eligibility rules

You are receiving a qualifying social welfare payment

You must be receiving Jobseeker's Allowance, Jobseeker's Benefit, One-Parent Family Payment, Disability Allowance, Back to Education Allowance, or another qualifying DSP payment. You cannot apply if you are not receiving any social welfare payment.

You have a genuine housing need

You must genuinely need rented accommodation. You cannot own your own home, have access to suitable accommodation, or be living with family who have space for you. The DSP will assess whether your need is genuine.

You have been renting for at least 6 months (the 6-month rule)

You must have been renting as your normal place of residence for at least 6 months in the 12 months before applying — in private rented accommodation, not social housing. There are exceptions for urgent cases and certain vulnerable groups.

Your income is below the means test limit

Your total household income must be below the means test threshold for your household size. The DSP assesses all household income including earnings, social welfare payments, maintenance, and savings above €5,000.

The 6-month rule — exceptions

The 6-month rule has important exceptions. You may be exempt if you are leaving direct provision or emergency accommodation, fleeing domestic violence or abuse, leaving an institution (hospital, prison), or if you have an urgent housing need assessed by your CWO. If you're unsure whether an exception applies to you, talk to your community welfare officer directly.

New arrivals from UkraineUkrainians under Temporary Protection are exempt from the 6-month rule and can apply for Rent Supplement without having previously rented in Ireland.

Who does NOT qualify

Full-time workers (30+ hours per week)

If you or your partner works 30 hours or more per week, you are not eligible for Rent Supplement. This is an absolute rule. Consider HAP instead, which is open to full-time workers.

People who own their home

If you own a home — even if it is not your current residence — you do not qualify unless there is a genuine reason you cannot live there (e.g. relationship breakdown, unsafe conditions).

People renting from family members

You cannot claim Rent Supplement for accommodation rented from a parent, sibling, or other close family member.

Students in full-time education

Full-time students are generally not eligible for Rent Supplement, with limited exceptions. Students on Back to Education Allowance may qualify — check with your CWO.

Working part-time? You may still qualify.Part-time workers can be eligible for Rent Supplement, but your earnings will affect how much you receive. See the guide for working part-time.
